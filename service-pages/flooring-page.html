FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

What types of home installations do you offer?

A: We install a wide range of household fixtures — from ceiling fans, light fixtures, and chandeliers to TVs, wall mounts, kitchen appliances, bathroom hardware, and more. We also handle smart home device setups, garage door openers, and outdoor lighting. If it’s part of your home and needs safe, reliable installation, we can take care of it.

Can you mount TVs and sound systems?

A: Absolutely. We securely mount TVs on drywall, brick, or stone surfaces, hiding cords where possible for a clean, modern look. We also install wall-mounted soundbars and surround-sound systems, ensuring everything is level, secure, and connected correctly.

Can you replace bathroom or kitchen fixtures?

A: Yes — we can install faucets, sinks, showerheads, towel bars, and other fixtures. We ensure all connections are tight and leak-free, and we can also assist with sealing or caulking to maintain a clean finish.

What should I do before an installation appointment?

A: Please clear the work area of personal items and furniture where possible. If it’s an electrical or plumbing install, ensure we have access to the breaker panel or shutoff valve. We’ll take care of the rest, including all tools, materials, and clean-up.

Can you install wood flooring over existing floors?

A: In many cases, yes — engineered or laminate wood flooring can be installed over existing tile, vinyl, or concrete as long as the surface is clean, level, and stable. We’ll inspect your current floor first to make sure it’s a suitable base. For solid hardwood, we usually recommend removing the old flooring to ensure proper fastening and long-term durability.

What types of wood flooring do you install?

A: We install a wide range of wood flooring options, including solid hardwood, engineered wood, and laminate. Solid hardwood offers timeless beauty and can be refinished multiple times, while engineered wood is ideal for areas that experience temperature or humidity changes — perfect for Lethbridge homes. Laminate flooring is a durable and budget-friendly option that still gives the look of real wood. We can help you choose the best type based on your home’s style, traffic, and climate.
